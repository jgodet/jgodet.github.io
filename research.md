## Current Research

My research is conducted within the [iCube laboratory](https://icube.unistra.fr) (UMR CNRS 7753), team **IMAGeS** — *Images, Modelling, Learning, Geometry and Statistics*, at the University of Strasbourg.

I focus on **statistical methods for observational health data**, with particular interest in leveraging data from hospital data warehouses for clinical research.

### Research Interests

- **Causal inference** in observational health data
- **Statistical modelling** of real-world / EHR data
- **Deep learning** applied to health data
- **Clinical research methodology & biostatistics**

### Current Project :

**DOME** (Data, Observational Methods and digital Epidemiology) is a project focused on the analysis of real-world health data, with the goal of producing rigorous analytical tools and frameworks adapted to the specificities of observational health data and directly applicable to digital (pharmaco-)epidemiology.

Two main axes:

- **Assessing real-world treatment effectiveness through causal approaches**
  
This axis focuses on assessing real-world treatment effectiveness including Target Trial Emulation (TTE) — an approach that allows rigorous evaluation of treatment effects from observational data when randomized controlled trials are ethically or practically unfeasible. The work in this axis includes developing and using methods for confounding control, propensity score-based adjustment (IPTW, IPCW, TMLE, doubly-robust estimators), and sensitivity analyses tailored to real-world data from medico-administrative databases and hospital data warehouses.
  
- **AI-Driven Drug Safety: Leveraging Non-Traditional Data Sources for Drug Safety in Vulnerable Populations**

This axis applies machine learning and AI to drug safety, including the quantitative detection of adverse drug events and the optimization of deprescribing. It leverages non-traditional data sources — hospital information system logs, online patient reviews, medication reconciliation records — to identify patients at high iatrogenic risk, with a particular focus on vulnerable populations such as elderly polymedicated patients.
